How Addiction Damages Your Body

  • Cardiovascular disease and heart attacks
  • Liver damage and cirrhosis
  • Respiratory problems and lung disease
  • Weakened immune system
  • Increased risk of cancer
  • Kidney failure and organ damage
  • Malnutrition and vitamin deficiencies

Mental Health Impact

Psychological Consequences

  • Depression and anxiety disorders
  • Memory loss and cognitive decline
  • Paranoia and psychosis
  • Mood swings and emotional instability
  • Increased suicide risk
  • Sleep disorders and insomnia
  • Loss of motivation and pleasure
  • Personality changes

Recovery Steps

1. Acknowledge the Problem

Recognize and admit that you have an addiction. This is the crucial first step to recovery.

2. Seek Professional Help

Contact healthcare providers, counselors, or addiction specialists for proper guidance.

3. Detoxification

Undergo medically supervised detox to safely manage withdrawal symptoms.

4. Rehabilitation Program

Participate in inpatient or outpatient treatment programs tailored to your needs.

5. Ongoing Support

Join support groups like AA, NA, or counseling for continued recovery assistance.

6. Lifestyle Changes

Develop new habits, relationships, and routines that support a substance-free life.
